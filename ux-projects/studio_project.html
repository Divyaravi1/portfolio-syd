TSG Quest

The Tin Sheds Gallery, originally a vibrant center for community engagement and political activism, has evolved since its inception in 1969, becoming part of the Sydney School of Architecture, Design, and Planning. Despite its historical significance, the gallery's community engagement has waned over time. To revitalize its relevance, a new housing-themed system has been developed using the gallery's archives. This system aims to foster open dialogue, connecting audiences and enhancing personal connections to the archives and the gallery. By addressing past, present, and future housing challenges, this initiative seeks to reinvigorate community engagement and relevance.


The goal

How can we use the past exhibitions about the evolution of housing and potential for design interventions showcased in the gallery to create a dialogue among the youth, invite their participation and collaborate with the architecture and design communities? Previous Iterations

We conducted comprehensive user testing on our initial iteration, gathering valuable insights and feedback. Through meticulous analysis of this data, we iteratively refined our approach to meet user needs and preferences. This informed our final iteration, ensuring it aligns seamlessly with user expectations and enhances overall user experience.
The Roofs was our intitial iteration, which was a compilation of multiple events (including an event called the TSG Quest, which was later adapted to our final concept). We tested the "Timescape" event for the user testing event that was held as a part of our unit.

Concept Iterations

In response to our formal user testing findings, how do we modify our design concept to promote deeper engagement with the archives and other users, while still ensuring the user experience is enjoyable and not too difficult? How can our concept foster both the comfort-level and creative spark needed for that deeper engagement to occur? How do we ensure each component has a clear purpose and that the purpose and related expectations are clearly conveyed to users?

Final Concept

By integrating the key insights garnered from the Bodystorming session into our concept, we aim to enrich discussions surrounding past, present, and future housing issues as well as the TSG archives among players. Our goal is to ensure that these discussions are not only informative but also enjoyable, enhancing the overall gameplay experience.
Our focus lies in providing additional context for the archive, ensuring seamless accessibility for users to engage with TSG QUEST. Through this approach, we aim to enhance user interaction and facilitate a deeper understanding of the archive's content and significance.

Website Prototype

To structure our audience effectively, we developed the housing archetype quiz with the aim of categorizing users into three distinct archetypes. These archetypes were determined through an online quiz available on our website. The quiz was designed in a manner that allows users to quickly answer five questions interactively to define their housing archetype. The quiz also serves as the primary gateway for users to sign up for the TSG QUEST.

FlowChart

This flowchart illustrates the step-by-step journey of a participant engaging with the TSG QUEST card game at the Tin Sheds Gallery. It tracks their experience from discovering the game online to actively participating in a game session and contributing to the TIMESCAPE. The flowchart visualizes key stages, including registration, game preparation, interactive gameplay, and post-game reflections. By outlining this process, the flowchart highlights the seamless user flow and the various touchpoints designed to foster engagement, learning, and community connections through the TSG QUEST experience.

StoryBoard

The storyboard depicts the journey of an individual participating in the TSG QUEST card game at the Tin Sheds Gallery. It begins with their discovery of the game on the TSG website and their decision to join a game session. At the gallery, they meet other participants, receive their game materials, and engage in exploring housing-related archives through the game. The storyboard highlights their interactions, challenges, and the connections they make with others. It concludes with the individual reflecting on their experience and contributing their thoughts to the TIMESCAPE, enhancing their connection to the community and the gallery's mission.
